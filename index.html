Digital Presence & Competitor Analysis

Where SecureMojo stands today, how we compare to the market, and what to do next — platform by platform.

📋 Research scope: 7 competitors manually audited across Instagram, LinkedIn, X, YouTube, Facebook, Threads & websites — Aura, Mitigata, NordVPN, ProtonVPN, Norton LifeLock, Incogni + SecureMojo full audit including Ben's personal brand & CyberPulse community
Core Finding
SecureMojo has a solid brand identity and consistent educational content — but 80% of what we publish teaches and only 5% connects emotionally. The fastest-growing players in this space (Aura, Mitigata, Incogni) all do one thing differently: they make audiences feel something first, then learn. That shift is the single most important thing to fix.

Section 06 — Platform-by-Platform Content Strategy

What works on each platform is fundamentally different. Same post mirrored everywhere is the biggest waste of effort in social media.

📸
Instagram
Current: 94 followers · 84 posts · ~6 avg likes. Highest potential for organic growth if format shifts.
What Works (from research)
  • Reels — Aura's street-interview reels get 52–66 likes on IG but 1K+ on YT. Reels are discovery.
  • Memes — Aura runs interactive memes ("me staring at login screen") — low effort, high shareability and save rates.
  • Consistent brand aesthetics — sticking to curated color schemes to create an instant premium profile feel.
What Needs Fixing
  • Content is heavily text-based and educational; does not inspire sharing or conversation.
  • No personal or behind-the-scenes content that showcases the team or makes users care.
  • Direct mirrored posts from Facebook ignoring IG-native aspect ratios and formats.
Action Playbook
  • Shift content ratio: 40% educational reels, 30% relatable cyber memes, 30% high-touch client support stories.
  • Launch a weekly "Mojo Quick-Tip" short reel series focusing on common phone/app privacy settings.
  • Collaborate with 2-3 Indian lifestyle micro-influencers to post joint security health checks.
💼
LinkedIn (Company)
Current: 246 followers · ~7 avg likes. Strong B2B trust-builder and corporate distribution node.
What Works (from research)
  • Team milestones, welcome posts, and office culture (e.g. Mitigata welcome posts getting 200+ likes).
  • Long-form thought leadership and professional company updates.
  • Structured infographics mapping executive and employee cyber risk.
What Needs Fixing
  • Low publishing frequency and lack of direct thought sharing.
  • The company page is isolated and doesn't pull reach from Ben's strong personal network.
  • Informal employee sharing; no system to amplify or comment on release posts.
Action Playbook
  • Cross-post Benild's highest-performing personal thought leadership pieces directly to the company page.
  • Initiate a weekly "Milestone Monday" showing team activities, onboarding, and case breakdowns.
  • Create a formal employee advocacy loop: all 13 team members amplify company releases within the first hour.
𝕏
X / Twitter
Current: 9 followers · 91 posts. Flat reach due to incorrect thread formatting and lack of hooks.
What Works (from research)
  • Incogni's hook-first threads and data statistics (reaching 100k+ views with only 7k followers).
  • Real-time commentary on active global security breaches.
  • Interactive polls (NordVPN getting 12K+ votes) used to engage and capture user opinions.
What Needs Fixing
  • Posts are flat text links or stock graphic cards that do not trigger the X recommendation engine.
  • Lack of conversational hooks and threads to explain complex breaches simply.
  • Zero interaction with other cybersecurity accounts, journalists, or tech founders.
Action Playbook
  • Convert long-form news bulletins into 5-post X threads starting with a high-friction visual hook.
  • Post weekly interactive polls analyzing public cybersecurity habits (e.g. "Do you reuse passwords?").
  • Spend 15 mins daily engaging in the replies of trending tech news accounts with white-hat analyst insights.
📺
YouTube
Current: No channel exists. The largest discovery gap relative to all major competitor baselines.
What Works (from research)
  • Aura's short street-interviews (1K+ views) repurposed as Shorts.
  • Explainer videos and deep-dives explaining real-world security risks (Nord/Norton).
  • People-centric thumbnails with high-contrast text hooks instead of stock graphics.
What Needs Fixing
  • Total absence of long-tail search visibility on the world's second largest search engine.
  • No repository of video walkthroughs or guides demonstrating how SecureMojo Advice works.
Action Playbook
  • Create the SecureMojo channel and upload a 2-minute explainer showing the WhatsApp concierge interface in action.
  • Produce 5 short-form Shorts weekly repurposing Ben's thought leadership insights.
  • Optimize title tagging around long-tail user queries (e.g. "How to recover hacked WhatsApp account").
👥
Facebook & Threads
Current: 19 posts / 1 follower. Minimal reach. Ignored community-building features.
What Works (from research)
  • Localized safety discussion groups and parent-focused protection guidelines (Norton).
  • Short, casual community conversations and text-only daily updates (Threads).
What Needs Fixing
  • Auto-mirroring Instagram posts directly to Facebook, which ruins text layout and link tags.
  • Complete lack of publishing or interaction on Threads.
Action Playbook
  • Limit Facebook to a clean business contact portal and post only major company announcements.
  • Utilize Threads as a casual thought journal, echoing X's short insights but in a conversational tone.
Section 07 — Actionable Recommendations for Founder's Office
01
Shift to Hook-First Content Mix
Rebalance current content from 80% education to 40% education/awareness, 30% relatable human/culture stories, and 30% concrete product/solution highlights. Prioritize emotional and shareable hooks that generate organic word-of-mouth.
02
Launch YouTube Search Footprint
Establish a presence on YouTube. Start with short, high-concept Shorts explaining common OSINT footprints, and publish a 3-minute video showing how SecureMojo Advice functions over WhatsApp to drive conversions.
03
Establish Systematic Employee Advocacy
Activate the employee network. Standardize post amplification so that all team members comment and share company page updates within 60 minutes of release to trigger the LinkedIn feed algorithm.
04
Scale CyberPulse Community
Deepen engagement in the CyberPulse LinkedIn group. Move away from admin-only updates to peer discussions, inviting members to submit recent scam messages for expert walkthroughs.
Section 08 — Engagement Hook Formulas
Exposure Hook
[Public Figure Name] + [How they got hacked] + [1 simple way to prevent it]
"How a tech founder's public location check-in cost them ₹12 Lakhs in under 5 minutes..."
Scam Triage Hook
[Immediate threat] + [Psychological trigger] + [What SecureMojo Advice did]
"\"Your Netflix account is locked.\" This text message fooled 10,000 users yesterday. Here's why."
POV Hook
[Hacker POV] + [Common daily habit] + [Hidden security exploit]
"A white-hat hacker looks at your public Wi-Fi habits. Here's what I see..."
Section 09 — Core Content Pillars
Pillar 01
The Hacker's Eye (POV)
Explaining security vulnerabilities from an offensive standpoint to make it approachable for normal users.
Examples: "How hackers search for your phone number; OSINT mapping teardowns."
Pillar 02
Survival Checklists
Highly actionable, short, step-by-step checklists to handle or mitigate active digital security threats.
Examples: "The 10-minute account recovery playbook; how to secure your WhatsApp."
Pillar 03
Scam Education
Busting common cybersecurity myths and explaining the psychology behind advanced social engineering.
Examples: "Why two-factor SMS is unsafe; how lookalike domains fool smart users."
