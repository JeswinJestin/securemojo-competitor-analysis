✓ What's Working Well

Our brand assets (green, black, purple) are consistently recognized. The Mojo Bulletin (15 editions) maintains high-quality subscriptions, and Ben's LinkedIn presence (TEDx speaker, hacker-to-founder) gives us a distinct trust builder. × Urgent Friction Areas

The actual product remains largely invisible on our socials — users learn the threat but don't see what SecureMojo does about it. X and Facebook are just mirrored feeds showing flat links that fail algorithms, and we have zero YouTube search footprint.

Ecosystem Reach & Amplification Assets

The company page has 246 followers, but our people-driven assets have a far wider footprint. Activating them systematically is free leverage.

Strengths ⚡
  • Human-in-the-loop: WhatsApp access creates unique, scaling trust.
  • Zero-friction UX: Audits run without installing clunky local apps.
  • Niche Positioning: motivated high-risk buyer profile ( VIP creators/founders).
Weaknesses ⚠️
  • Scale logistics: WhatsApp response speeds rely on expert staff.
  • Sticker Shock: ₹6,000/year upfront price lacks a lower trial tier.
  • Zero virality: Content explains tech definitions instead of sharing stories.
Opportunities 💡
  • B2B2C Channels: Pitch bulk licenses directly to VC portfolios and talent managers.
  • AI Triage Layer: Auto-respond basic links via WhatsApp, route exploits to experts.
  • Freemium Check: Let users run a quick dark web scan on lander to hook emails.
Threats 🔥
  • Feature catching: Antivirus giants adding human advisors to standard subscriptions.
  • Privacy trust: Deeper OSINT checks require impeccable security reputation.

Platform-by-Platform Playbooks

Granular strategies to capture attention, reformat our content, and grow engagement locally.

What Works

Mitigata VP welcome post got 246+ likes. Culture content and sports outings (160+ likes) outperform dry product explainers 5x. People engage with people.

Gaps to Fix

The company page is isolated from Ben's strong 17K network. We lack team highlights, employee milestones, and structured organic amplification.

Strategy

Cross-post Benild's strong personal thought pieces to the company feed. Introduce office culture snapshots and team stories weekly.

💼 LinkedIn Execution Checklist
What Works

Aura's short reels (street-interviews asking public about cyber habits) gain viral loops. Relatable cyber memes establish regular profile shareability.

Gaps to Fix

Current posts are textbook explainers ("What is HTTPS"). High quality, but static and unshareable. No human faces or video formats.

Strategy

Shift grid from strict lessons to relatable memes + street-interviews. Add human-centric video walkthroughs showing Mojo Advice triage.

📸 Instagram Execution Checklist
What Works

Incogni's hook-first threads. Starting with shocking statistics or public hacks gets 100K+ impressions with a small following. Interactive polls work well.

Gaps to Fix

We publish flat link mirrors and stock cards. These receive zero algorithm boosts. We don't interact with tech journalists or security reporters in replies.

Strategy

Repurpose Mojo Bulletin news into native text-only X threads with high-friction hook setups. Use polls to drive interaction loops.

𝕏 X / Twitter Execution Checklist
What Works

Explainer guides answering search queries ("how to secure WhatsApp account"). High-contrast visual thumbnails featuring real people.

Gaps to Fix

Total absence of a channel. Missed opportunity for long-tail SEO on the second largest search engine. No product walk-through archives.

Strategy

Launch channel focusing on short walkthroughs and security tutorials. Repurpose Benild's key thoughts as Shorts.

📺 YouTube Execution Checklist
What Works

Parent-focused security guidelines and family data safety posts. Casual, daily text journals on Threads.

Gaps to Fix

Automatic mirroring from IG to FB ruins formatting, link layouts, and hashtags. Zero posts or interactions on Threads.

Strategy

Maintain FB strictly as a basic business profile card. Experiment with Threads as a thought dump for quick cyber facts.
